— A letter from the maker

Why a small press, in the year 2026.

I started omnigoods because there is too much of everything, and most of it is too fast.

I spent my working life making things — slowly, mostly, and by hand when I could. I watched the world get faster, then thinner, then so thin you could see right through it. I waited for someone else to write the book I wanted to read, and at some point I noticed that I was the someone.

So: a small press. One title at a time, written slowly, edited slowly, sold direct, kept honest. No subscriptions. No bundles you don't want. No surveillance. You buy a book, you keep a book, and I move on to the next one.

If you find one here that earns its place on your shelf, that is enough.

— [Your name], proprietor
